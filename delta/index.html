Victoria, British Columbia

Places to Visit

  • Butchart Gardens: Located just outside of Victoria, Butchart Gardens have been entertaining visitors for over 100 years. Comprised of 55 acres of gardens with 900 bedding plant varieties, the Gardens offer visitors different varieties of blooming plants and the resulting vivid colors that change depending on the season. The oldest section of the Gardens is the Sunken Garden which was started in a former limestone quarry by the Butchart family who owned the mine. During the day you could see the colors of the flowers while during summer evenings you could see the illuminated plants. For children, there is the hand-carved Rose Carousel in the Childrens Pavillion. There are also events held at the Gardens. During the summer, symphony orchestras, rock bands and global acts perform at the Gardens. Visit Website
  • Inner Harbour: The Inner Harbour is part of the Victoria Harbour - one of the most beautiful harbors in the world. Small cruise ships as well as recreation vessels can moor in the Inner Harbour and visitors can disembark and visit the center of Victoria which is just steps away. During the summer, festivals, shows and music performances are held at the Inner Harbour. The Inner Harbour is the home of whale watching and ecotourism businesses and is also the home of the International Ferry Terminal with ferry connections to Port Angeles, Bellingham and Seattle. Visit Website

  • Royal BC Museum: (To see picture, click on Inner Harbour picture) Founded in 1886 and voted the best museum in Canada five times by Trip Advisor users, the Royal BC Museum is a world-class museum of natural and human history. Among the natural history galleries you can see a wooly mammoth in its natural surroundings while among the human history galleries you can see totems, masks and other cultural treasures at the First Peoples Gallery In addition to its permanent exhibits, the Museum also has touring exhibits which recently included HMS Titanic artifacts and Egyptian artifacts. The IMAX Victoria theater within the Museum shows educational films as well as regular commercial movies. Visit Website
